Peptide Reconstitution Guide

Step-by-step guide to safely reconstituting lyophilised peptide vials. For research purposes only.

Lyophilised (freeze-dried) peptides must be reconstituted with bacteriostatic water (BAC water) before use. This process requires care and sterile technique to preserve peptide integrity and prevent contamination. Follow these steps precisely.

⚠️
Important: Always use bacteriostatic water (BAC water) — not sterile water or saline — for reconstitution. BAC water contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol as a preservative, allowing multi-dose use of the reconstituted vial. Never shake the vial. Never inject water directly onto the powder pellet. Store reconstituted vials refrigerated at 2–8°C and use within the compound-specific timeframe.
01
Gather Your Supplies
You will need: the peptide vial, bacteriostatic water (BAC water), insulin syringes (100 unit / 1mL), alcohol swabs, and a clean flat surface. Wash hands thoroughly before starting.
02
Sterilise the Tops
Wipe the rubber stopper on both the peptide vial and the BAC water vial with a fresh alcohol swab. Allow to air dry for at least 30 seconds. Do not touch the stoppers after wiping.
03
Draw BAC Water
Insert the syringe needle into the BAC water vial and draw up the required amount. Refer to your personalised dosing card for the exact volume — typically 1–2mL depending on the compound.
04
Add Water to Vial
Insert the needle into the peptide vial and angle it so water runs slowly down the inside glass wall — never directly onto the powder pellet. Inject slowly. Do not force it.
05
Dissolve Gently
Once water is added, gently swirl the vial between your palms in a circular motion. Do not shake, vortex, or tap aggressively. The powder should dissolve within 30–90 seconds into a clear solution.
06
Inspect & Store
The reconstituted solution should be clear and colourless. Discard if cloudy, discoloured, or contains visible particles. Label the vial with the date. Refrigerate immediately. Do not freeze.

What is bacteriostatic water and why do I need it?+
Bacteriostatic water (BAC water) is sterile water containing 0.9% benzyl alcohol. The benzyl alcohol acts as a preservative, inhibiting bacterial growth in the reconstituted vial and allowing safe multi-dose use over 28–60 days. Never substitute with plain sterile water or saline for multi-dose use — these have no preservative and are only suitable for single-use reconstitution.
How do I store reconstituted peptides?+
Reconstituted peptides should be refrigerated at 2–8°C immediately after preparation. Do not freeze reconstituted vials as this can damage the peptide structure. Unreconstituted (lyophilised) vials can be stored at room temperature away from heat and light, or refrigerated for extended shelf life. Always check compound-specific storage guidance on your dosing card.
